About AMRUTHA

AMRUTHA Welfare Society is a non-profit, non-governmental, non-political, and non-religious organization established in 2004 by a group of grassroots women development professionals. We drive sustainable and inclusive growth through community-driven participation.

We operate across **Paderu, G. Madugula, and Hukumpeta mandals** of Alluri Sitarama Raju District, Andhra Pradesh, reaching **116 villages** and supporting **over 3,750 tribal families**. Our approach emphasizes self-reliance, social awareness, and democratic values among tribal, dalit, and rural communities, with a special focus on women and children.

Our Core Values

  • Championing the right to self-organization for tribal communities.
  • Fostering unity, cohesion, and collective action for self-reliance.
  • Operating as a non-political, secular, and action-oriented catalyst.

Vision & Mission

Our Vision

A just, inclusive, and sustainable tribal society in the Eastern Ghats where women, children, and marginalized communities live with dignity, self-reliance, and harmony with nature through the protection of their natural resources and cultural heritage.

Our Mission

To empower communities to build self-confidence and self-reliance; to support them in protecting, conserving, and regenerating their natural resources; and to foster comprehensive development by strengthening the cultural, socio-economic, and ecological links between people and their environment.

Our Core Focus Areas

Integrated strategies for sustainable development and resilience.

Sustainable Livelihoods

  • **Climate-Smart Agriculture:** Promoting ZBNF, SRI, and Millets.
  • **Land Development:** Reclamation of wastelands through soil/water conservation and contour bunding.
  • **Nutritional Security:** Vegetable cultivation, Kitchen gardens, and Nutri-gardens.
  • **Value Addition:** Community-operated processing centers and skill training for market access (Marts).

Resource & Environment

  • **Biodiversity:** Protection and regeneration of native plant species and mixed cropping.
  • **Nursery-Based Regeneration:** Community nurseries for fruit, timber, fodder, and medicinal plants.
  • **Climate Adaptation:** Promoting agroforestry systems to restore soil health and sequester carbon.
  • **Water Solutions:** Facilitating gravity-based water structures for drinking and irrigation.

Community Empowerment

  • **Health & Hygiene:** Conducting general, dental, and eye camps; Poshan camps; and waste management systems.
  • **Traditional Healing:** Maintaining a **Tribal Healers Network** to preserve traditional practices.
  • **FPO Promotion:** Supporting three **Farmer Producer Organizations** (Jodla, Vaishaki Siridhaanyalu, Farm From Forest) for collective action and market power.
  • **Skill Development:** Providing entrepreneurship and skill training.
